Intellectual Property Development Services

The role of IP protection in developing new technologies and products is absolutely critical. We support intellectual property development through our deeply specialized subject matter expertise and industry knowledge of high technologies and sciences. Under the IP management umbrella we provide patent search and patent drafting services, patent application assistance, patent prosecution support, and patent portfolio maintenance.  

Our team of highly qualified experts can bring their knowledge of the USPTO and other patent offices, intellectual property processes and specific technology expertise to help develop a strong IP strategy.

Patent Search

Conducting patentability analyses and developing design-around strategies allow our clients to avoid unneeded invention filings and to direct their R&D budgets in a more efficient way.

These patent searches may include a patentability search, a patent validity (or invalidity) search, an FTO or clearance search, or a state-of-the-art (landscape) patent search.

Patent Drafting

High quality patent drafting services must focus on acquiring durable patents in the US that afford commercial advantage and withstand third-party challenges.

The patent drafting services we offer our clients include our expertise in developing, drafting, filing and prosecuting patents in a variety of technical and scientific fields.

Patent Application

Patent applications are reviewed by patent offices. Usually governmental bodies, they have the power to accept or reject any patent application in their jurisdiction.

When you know what to expect during the approval process, you can save a significant amount of time by reducing any chances of rejection.

Patent Prosecution

Patent prosecution is at the heart of IP protection in developing new technologies and products. We support intellectual property development through our deeply specialized subject matter expertise.

We provide experts with deep industry and technology experience prior to litigation as well as during trials as both consulting experts and expert witnesses that testify during IP cases.

Patent Portfolio Analysis

IP professionals understand that portfolios of patents are commonplace and they require detailed examination of competitors, acquisition targets, and internal assets.

The primary purposes of most portfolio analyses include: gap and whitespace identification, potential infringements, and portfolio optimization and diversity.
